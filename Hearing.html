Hearing wood and seeing metal

Abstract

Knocking sounds and visual material stimuli have been found to efficiently convey emotions separately. This study aims to examine the influence of different door materials, presented visually, on a knocking sound’s perceived emotion. Using wooden knocking sounds with predetermined conveyed emotions paired with images of doors of different materials and variations, an online survey was conducted to examine a possible change in perceived emotions based on this incongruence. The results found no evidence of influence. For a more generalizable conclusion, the study identified a need for more control over the subjects’ testing environment.
